DOUGLAS ENGELBART

The man who invented the GUI



The early computing days were littered with command-line interfaces, and the systems were nearly unusable for the everyday person unless you had the time to memorize dozens of commands. Douglas Engelbart and his small team of engineers at Stanford Research Institute developed the oN-Line System, or NLS, which featured the "mouse", basic windows, word processing, hypertext, and even video conferencing among other things for the first time during his public computer demonstration at the Fall Joint Computer Conference in San Francisco on December 9, 1968.
