DOUGLAS ENGELBART

Douglas Engelbart and his small team of engineers at Stanford Research Institute developed the oN-Line System, or NLS, which featured the "mouse", windows, word processing, and hypertext among other things for the first time during his computer demonstration at the Fall Joint Computer Conference in San Francisco on December 9, 1968.

Then Xerox Parc revolutionized Engelbart's work.
