SMALLTALK

The next big step for the graphical user interface



During the 1970's, the company Xerox formed the Palo Alto Research Center, allowing people to have free reign on developing anything they wanted. One product that resulted was the Xerox Alto computer which came with a keyboard and mouse which manipulated the computer's interface, Smalltalk. This interface introduced individualized windows that can overlap, menus, icons, scrollbars, and more. The Smalltalk interface set the foundations for the modern-day graphical user interface.


The GUI displayed on the Xerox Alto


Apple's Steve Jobs visited the Xerox PARC facility in 1979. He was so impressed by Smalltalk that he immediately wanted his company's computers to feature their own GUIs.
